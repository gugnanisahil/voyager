ICONIC PLACES TO VISIT IN CANADA

Ride a Gondola



Banff, Alberta is home to a few Gondola experiences which will have you taking in amazing views while having a rush of an experience at the same time.


Gondola #1 – The Banff Gondola


The most famous of the Gondolas, The Banff Gondola is a glass-enclosed 8-minute ride that will take you up Sulphur Mountain and drop you off at one of the best viewpoints in the whole park. At the top, you’ll find an Interpretive Centre, fantastic restaurants, and a scenic boardwalk. The Banff Gondola is located in Banff at the end of Mountain Avenue. If you don’t have a car, then you can easily get to it by taking the Bow Valley ROAM Transit bus, on Route 1. This gondola is easily one of the most famous things to do in Banff!


Gondola #2 – The Lake Louise Gondola


A 14-minute ride located at the Lake Louise Ski Resort, the Lake Louise Gondola gives you two riding options: a classic enclosed gondola experience, or an open-air chairlift ride. When you get to the top (2088m / 6850 ft), there are restaurants, coffee bars, and views to soak up.


Gondola #3 – Banff Norquay Sightseeing Chairlift


While not a gondola per se, the Banff Norquay Sightseeing Chairlift is a 15-minute ride that will give you an entirely new perspective of Banff. From the chairlift, you’ll get views of the Town of Banff, Bow River, and the towering cliffs of Mt. Rundle. When the chairlift gets to the top, you’ll find the Cliff House Bistro where you can have a meal or a drink and take in the magnificent views.


Explore the Town of Banff



If you’re looking for a nature break and want a relatively more urban experience for a day, then consider all that downtown Banff has to offer. No matter your budget, there are plenty of attractions in Banff including shopping, restaurants, art galleries, and museums.

Speaking of museums, if you want to learn a little bit more about the Rocky Mountains then check out the Banff Park Museum, the Whyte Museum of the Canadian Rockies, or the Buffalo Nations Luxton Museum.

As for Banff art galleries, The Carter – Ryan gallery is not to be missed. Showcasing the art of indigenous artist Jason Carter, this art gallery is a staple in Canmore and the town of Banff.

Additionally, if you’re looking for something a little different, I recommend paying a visit to the Banff Centre for Arts and Creativity where you’ll find art, festivals, concerts, and workshops.
